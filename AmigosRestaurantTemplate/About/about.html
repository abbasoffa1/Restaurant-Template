OUR HISTORY

The rapid growth of travel through the 19th Century thanks to railways and steamships meant more people began traveling greater distances. This, in turn, increased the need for restaurants. Building on the early success of fine dining restaurants in Paris, a new style of dining became the norm in Europe and the United States. Patrons dined at private tables, chose their meals from an a la carte menu and paid their check at the end of the meal.
